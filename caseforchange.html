The Case for Change

There are countless reasons why we are due for a change in the leadership of the Utah County Attorney's Office, and the following cases are just a small sampling of the kinds of issues that have arisen and that need to be addressed by bringing in a fresh perspective, new approaches, and broader private sector experience that puts the public first.


  1. Auditor's Report
    A report from the State Auditor's Office concludes that a County contract -- approved by the Utah County Attorney's office -- had deficiencies that failed to protect the county's investment.
  2. Overly-aggressive Prosecution--Craig Case
    An aggressive prosecution, without "one bit of evidence" (according to the judge), destroys the livelihood of a former Miss Utah.
  3. Ignoring Court Orders--MacNeill Case
    A Real Risk of Reversal on Appeal or New Trial Because of Prosecutors Ignoring Court Orders in the MacNeill Murder Trial.
  4. More Ignoring Court of Orders--Pratt Case
    Prosecutor's Refusals to Comply with Judge's Order Leads to Dismissal of Securities Fraud Case
  5. Closing Arguments--Mortensen Case
    The County Attorney's Office has made some inaccurate criticisms of this campaign. Each email I have sent has been accompanied by legal documentation or has been a fully-attributed, first-hand account. Included in this particular email was the documentation for the Mortensen case, where two individuals were wrongfully charged with murder by the County Attorney.
